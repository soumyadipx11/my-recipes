Chicken Biryani

Description

Biryani is a fragrant, flavorful, and richly spiced rice dish popular in South Asia, especially India, Pakistan, and Bangladesh. It is made by layering partially cooked rice with marinated meat (chicken, mutton, beef, or fish) or vegetables, along with aromatic spices, fried onions, and herbs. The dish is slow-cooked ("dum") to allow the flavors to blend perfectly.

Ingredients

For the Rice:

  • 2 cups basmati rice (soaked 30 minutes)
  • 4–5 cups water
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 3–4 green cardamoms
  • 1 black cardamom
  • 4–5 cloves
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 tsp salt

For the Chicken Marinade:

  • 500 g chicken (bone-in pieces preferred)
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder
  • 1–2 green chilies (slit)
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt to taste

Other Ingredients:

  • 2 medium onions (thinly sliced, fried till golden brown)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (chopped)
  • 2–3 tbsp cooking oil or ghee
  • A handful of fresh mint leaves
  • A handful of fresh coriander leaves
  • A pinch of saffron soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk (optional, for aroma and color)

Steps

Step 1: Prepare the Rice

  1. Boil water with whole spices and salt.
  2. Add soaked rice and cook until 70% done (grains should still be firm).
  3. Drain and set aside.

Step 2: Cook the Chicken

  1. Heat oil/ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan.
  2. Add marinated chicken and cook until it turns white and starts releasing oil.
  3. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  4. Mix in half of the fried onions, some mint, and coriander leaves.
  5. Simmer until chicken is almost cooked (80%).

Step 3: Layering the Biryani

  1. In a heavy-bottomed pot, spread a thin layer of cooked chicken at the bottom.
  2. Add a layer of rice on top.
  3. Sprinkle some fried onions, mint, coriander, and saffron milk.
  4. Repeat layers until all rice and chicken are used.
  5. Cover with a tight lid (or seal with dough for “dum” cooking).

Step 4: Dum Cooking

  1. Place the pot on very low heat (you can place it on a tawa/griddle to avoid burning).
  2. Cook for 20–25 minutes to let the flavors infuse.

Step 5: Serve

  • Gently fluff the biryani before serving.
  • Serve hot with raita (yogurt dip), salad, and boiled eggs (optional).
