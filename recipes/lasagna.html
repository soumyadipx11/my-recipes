Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made by layering wide, flat sheets of pasta with rich fillings such as meat, cheese, vegetables, and sauces. Traditionally, it features alternating layers of pasta, a savory meat sauce (often made with ground beef or pork simmered in tomato sauce), creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese, and grated mozzarella or Parmesan. Once assembled, it’s baked until the top is golden and bubbly, with the layers melding together into a hearty, comforting casserole.

Ingredients

For the meat sauce (ragù):

  • Olive oil
  • Onion (finely chopped)
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Ground beef (or a mix of beef and pork)
  • Tomato paste
  • Crushed tomatoes (or tomato sauce)
  • Red wine (optional, for depth)
  • Italian seasoning (oregano, basil, thyme)
  • Salt and black pepper
  • Fresh basil (optional)

For the cheese filling:

  • Ricotta cheese (or cottage cheese as an alternative)
  • Egg (to bind the ricotta mixture)
  • Fresh parsley (chopped)
  • Parmesan cheese (grated)

For the layers and topping:

  • Lasagna noodles (traditional sheets, either boiled or oven-ready)
  • Mozzarella cheese (shredded)
  • Parmesan cheese (extra, for topping)

Steps

Step 1: Make the meat sauce

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pan.
  2. Sauté onion until soft, then add garlic.
  3. Add ground beef (and/or pork) and cook until browned.
  4. Stir in tomato paste, crushed tomatoes, and (optional) red wine.
  5. Season with Italian herbs, salt, and pepper.
  6. Simmer 20–30 minutes until thickened, stirring occasionally.

Step 2: Prepare the cheese filling

  1. In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese with 1 egg, parsley, and some Parmesan.
  2. Season lightly with salt and pepper.

Step 3: Cook the pasta

  • If using regular lasagna sheets: boil until al dente, then drain.
  • If using oven-ready sheets: skip this step.

Step 4: Assemble the lasagna

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a baking dish, spread a thin layer of meat sauce.
  3. Layer pasta sheets over the sauce.
  4. Spread ricotta mixture on the pasta.
  5. Add mozzarella and a bit of Parmesan.
  6. Spoon over more meat sauce.
  7. Repeat layers (pasta → ricotta → mozzarella → sauce) until the dish is nearly full.
  8. Finish with pasta, sauce, and a generous layer of mozzarella + Parmesan.

Step 5: Bake

  1. Cover the dish loosely with foil (to prevent cheese from burning).
  2. Bake 25 minutes covered, then uncover and bake 20–25 minutes more until the top is golden and bubbly.

Step 6: Rest and serve

  • Let the lasagna rest 10–15 minutes before slicing, so the layers set nicely.
