Butter Chicken

Description

Butter Chicken is a rich and creamy North Indian dish made with tender chicken pieces simmered in a luscious tomato-based gravy. The chicken is marinated in yogurt and spices, grilled or pan-fried, then finished in a smooth sauce flavored with butter, cream, and aromatic spices. It has a mild, slightly sweet, and buttery taste, making it a favorite worldwide, especially when served with naan or steamed basmati rice.

Ingredients

For the Chicken Marinade:

  • 500 g boneless chicken (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt to taste

For the Gravy:

  • 3 tbsp butter (or mix of butter + oil)
  • 2 medium onions (chopped)
  • 3 medium tomatoes (blended into puree)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2–3 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • ½ cup cream (or milk + cashew paste for richness)
  • 1 tbsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves, crushed)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • Salt to taste

Steps

Step 1: Marinate the Chicken

  1. Mix chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, spices, lemon juice, and salt.
  2. Marinate for at least 1 hour (overnight gives the best flavor).

Step 2: Cook the Chicken

  1. Heat a little butter/oil in a pan.
  2. Grill or sauté the marinated chicken until almost cooked. Set aside.

Step 3: Prepare the Gravy

  1. In the same pan, add butter.
  2. Sauté onions until golden. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies.
  3. Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates.
  4. Mix in chili powder, turmeric, coriander, cumin, and salt. Cook for 2–3 minutes.

Step 4: Combine

  1. Add the cooked chicken into the gravy.
  2. Pour in cream, kasuri methi, and garam masala. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Adjust salt and spices as needed.

Step 5: Serve

  • Garnish with coriander leaves and a drizzle of cream.
  • Serve hot with naan, roti, or basmati rice.
